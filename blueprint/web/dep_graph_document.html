Definition 3Formal power series ring

For a commutative ring \(R\), let \(R\@undefined t\@undefined \) denote the ring of formal power series

\[ F(t)=\sum _{n\ge 0} a_n t^n,\qquad a_n\in R, \]

with the usual Cauchy product:

\[ (FG)(t)=\sum _{n\ge 0}\Big(\sum _{i=0}^n a_i b_{n-i}\Big)t^n. \]

Definition 7Lower convex hull

Let \(S\subset \mathbb {R}^2\). The lower convex hull \(\operatorname {LCH}(S)\) is the intersection of all closed convex sets \(C\subset \mathbb {R}^2\) such that:

  • \(S\subset C\);

  • \(C\) is downward closed in the vertical direction, i.e. if \((x,y)\in C\) and \(y'\ge y\) then \((x,y')\in C\).

Equivalently, \(\operatorname {LCH}(S)\) is the closed convex hull of \(S\) together with all vertical rays \(\{ (x,y')\mid y'\ge y\} \) above points \((x,y)\in S\).

Definition 9Newton points of a power series

Let \((K,v)\) be a non-archimedean valued field and \(F(t)=\sum _{n\ge 0} f_n t^n\in K\@undefined t\@undefined \). Define the set of Newton points of \(F\) by

\[ \mathcal{P}(F) := \{ (n, v(f_n)) \in \mathbb {R}^2 \mid n\in \mathbb {Z}_{\ge 0},\ f_n\neq 0\} . \]

(Coefficients \(f_n=0\) are omitted, since \(v(0)=\infty \).)

Definition 1Non-archimedean valuation

A non-archimedean valuation on a field \(K\) is a map

\[ v : K \to \mathbb {R}\cup \{ \infty \} \]

such that for all \(x,y\in K\):

  • \(v(0)=\infty \) and \(v(1)=0\);

  • \(v(xy)=v(x)+v(y)\);

  • \(v(x+y)\ge \min \{ v(x),v(y)\} \) (ultrametric inequality).

We call \((K,v)\) a non-archimedean valued field.

Definition 15Support functional for lower convex sets

For \(C\subset \mathbb {R}^2\) and \(\lambda \in \mathbb {R}\) define

\[ h_C(\lambda ) := \inf _{(x,y)\in C}\ (y+\lambda x)\ \in \ \mathbb {R}\cup \{ -\infty ,\infty \} . \]

When \(C\) is closed, convex, and vertically downward closed, the family \(\{ h_C(\lambda )\} _{\lambda \in \mathbb {R}}\) encodes the lower boundary of \(C\).

Let \((K,v)\) be non-archimedean and \(F(t)=\sum _{n\ge 0} f_n t^n\in K\@undefined t\@undefined \). For \(\lambda \in \mathbb {R}\) define the tropical weight

\[ w_F(\lambda ) := \inf _{n\ge 0}\ \big(v(f_n)+\lambda n\big)\ \in \ \mathbb {R}\cup \{ -\infty ,\infty \} . \]

Lemma 8Basic properties of Minkowski sums and lower hulls

Let \(A,B\subset \mathbb {R}^2\).

  1. If \(A\subset A'\) and \(B\subset B'\), then \(A+B\subset A'+B'\).

  2. If \(C,D\) are closed and convex, then \(C+D\) is closed and convex.

  3. If \(C,D\) are vertically downward closed, then \(C+D\) is vertically downward closed.

Lemma 11Newton polygon of the zero series

If \(F=0\) in \(K\@undefined t\@undefined \), then \(\mathcal{P}(F)=\varnothing \) and hence \(\mathrm{NP}(F)=\operatorname {LCH}(\varnothing )\). Theorem 21Product formula over \(\mathbb {Q}_p\)

Proposition. Let \(F(t) = \sum _{n \geq 0} f_n t^n\) and \(G(t) = \sum _{n \geq 0} g_n t^n\) be two power series in \(\mathbb {Q}_p\@undefined t\@undefined \). Then the Newton polygon of \(F(t)G(t)\) is the Minkowski sum of the Newton polygons of \(F(t)\) and \(G(t)\):

\[ \mathrm{NP}(F(t)G(t))\ =\ \mathrm{NP}(F(t))\ +\ \mathrm{NP}(G(t)). \]
